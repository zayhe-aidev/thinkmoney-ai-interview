# thinkmoney Policies

## Fair Usage Policy

thinkmoney accounts are designed for personal everyday banking. The following usage guidelines apply:

- **ATM withdrawals:** Up to 10 free ATM withdrawals per calendar month. Additional withdrawals incur a fee (see fees schedule).
- **Currency exchange:** No fair-usage limit on weekday exchanges. Weekend exchanges are subject to a higher markup.
- **Card transactions:** No transaction count limits for standard purchases.
- **Cash deposits:** Up to £1,000 per month via PayPoint locations. Amounts above this may require source-of-funds verification.

Accounts used primarily for commercial activity, cryptocurrency trading, or high-frequency currency speculation may be subject to review and potential closure.

## Acceptable Use Policy

thinkmoney accounts must not be used for:
- Money laundering or terrorist financing.
- Purchasing illegal goods or services.
- Gambling transactions exceeding £500 per month (self-exclusion tools are available in the app).
- Cryptocurrency purchases via third-party exchanges (direct crypto platforms are blocked).
- Operating a business without a thinkmoney Business account.
- Receiving proceeds of crime or facilitating fraud.

Violation of this policy may result in immediate account suspension and reporting to relevant authorities.

## Data Protection & GDPR

thinkmoney processes your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

**Your rights:**
- Access your personal data (Subject Access Request).
- Rectify inaccurate data.
- Erase your data (subject to legal retention requirements).
- Restrict or object to processing.
- Data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@thinkmoney.co.uk or via in-app chat.

We retain transaction data for 5 years after account closure, as required by Anti-Money Laundering regulations.

## Complaints Procedure

If you are unhappy with our service:

1. **Contact us first:** Raise your concern via in-app chat, phone, or email.
2. **We'll acknowledge:** Within 24 hours of receiving your complaint.
3. **Investigation:** We aim to resolve complaints within 15 business days. Complex cases may take up to 35 business days.
4. **Final response:** You will receive a written final response detailing our findings and any remedial action.
5. **Still unhappy?** If you are not satisfied with our final response, you can refer your complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) within 6 months. Visit www.financial-ombudsman.org.uk.

## Dormant Account Policy

An account is considered dormant if there has been no customer-initiated activity for 12 consecutive months. This includes no logins, transactions, or balance inquiries.

**What happens:**
- After 12 months of inactivity, we will attempt to contact you via email and SMS.
- If no response within 30 days, a monthly inactivity fee of £3.00 will be applied.
- After 24 months of total inactivity, we may close the account and send any remaining balance to the last known bank account on file.
- If we cannot return your funds, they will be held in a safeguarded suspense account for up to 6 years.
